Living along the West Michigan shoreline offers breathtaking views, but it also exposes your home to some of the most consistent and powerful wind loads in the Midwest. From Grand Haven to North Muskegon, coastal roofs must be built differently to prevent shingle blow-off, ice dams, and structural fatigue.

### The Coastal Wind Challenge

Standard roofing installations often fail prematurely on the lakeshore because they aren't designed for the uplift pressures generated by Lake Michigan storm fronts. When wind hits a roof, it creates a vacuum effect on the leeward side, trying to pull shingles off the deck.

### How We Engineer for the Shoreline

In our ROOFIT division, we don't just "shingle" a house—we build an integrated roofing system. Here is how we protect your investment:

1. **Enhanced Fastening Patterns:** We use a 6-nail fastening pattern as a baseline for lakeshore projects, significantly increasing the blow-off resistance compared to standard 4-nail patterns.
2. **High-Wind Certified Shingles:** We select shingle products with reinforced nail zones and high-velocity wind ratings (up to 130 MPH).
3. **Synthetic Underlayments:** Unlike traditional organic felt, our synthetic underlayments provide a secondary water barrier that won't tear or degrade under thermal expansion.

### Maintenance is Key

Even the best roof needs inspection. We recommend a professional evaluation every 2-3 years for lakeshore properties to check for loose ridge caps and sealants that may have been stressed by seasonal gale-force winds.
